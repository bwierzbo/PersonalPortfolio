Final Reflection

My time at Pauline’s Lucky Garden was educational, and impactful on how I’m thinking about my future career choices. I was hired as a farm intern where I was responsible for seeding, transplanting, cultivating organic crops as well as harvesting and packaging the crops to be sold locally. I had no previous knowledge of farming and now I have a lettuce bed and 6 tomato plants at home. I also handled several projects at Pauline’s farm, I restored an old rusted wheel barrow and I cleaned and organized the farm’s greenhouse. I learned how to handle multiple personal projects as well as work on daily tasks on the farm. This internship tested my time management to a new level taking up the mornings of three school days a week, I had to learn how stay productive even after hours of labor.

My other large contribution to Pauline’s Lucky Market Garden was building the farm’s first website using HTML, CSS, and Java Script. I worked with Pauline and Alyssa, a SU digital arts major and farm hand, to create a logo, take professional photos for the website, and design the website to best suit and promote Pauline’s Lucky Market Garden. This internship not only strengthen my web development skills but also my ability to work with others on a projects using their strengths to build up my own weaknesses.

Overall, my internship with Pauline's Lucky Market Garden was amazing. My biggest takeaway is discovering the line of work I want to be in. I now know that I have a passion for creating innovative solutions and working on increasing systems efficiency. Knowing this I have applyed to Embry Riddle Aeronautical University's systems engineering masters program. Pauline Copello, my internship supervisor, is an inspirational person, I aspire to be as hardworking and as dedicated as her to what I love. I recommend this internship to anyone who is interested in sustainable food systems, organic farming or someone who is wants to learn new things and is willing to put in the work.
